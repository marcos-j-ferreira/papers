# Machine Learning Papers

![Cover](./wallpaper-readme.jpg)

This repository contains my personal papers, notes, and hands-on in **Machine Learning**.
The goal is to document my learning journey, from fundamental concepts to deeper theoretical understanding, while also practicing how to explain complex ideas in a clear and structured way.

Instead of only consuming content, I focus on:

* Writing my own interpretations
* Building small practical examples
* Connecting theory with real-world applications

## Purpose

This repository is not just a collection of files — it represents a **learning system**.

Each paper aims to:

* Break down complex ML concepts into simple explanations
* Combine theory with intuition
* Reinforce knowledge through writing (one of the most effective ways to learn)

## Papers

###  1. Artificial Neuron & Perceptron

**File:** `neuronio_artificial_I__LLMs_pt.pdf`

This paper explores the foundations of Machine Learning through the **Perceptron**, one of the earliest models of artificial neurons.

Topics covered:

* Historical context and origins of the perceptron
* Mathematical intuition behind the perceptron
* Decision boundaries and linear classification
* Why the perceptron was a breakthrough — and its limitations

This work builds the conceptual bridge between simple linear models and modern neural networks.

---

### 2. Gradients and Optimization

**File:** `Gradients_en.pdf`

This paper focuses on one of the most important mechanisms in Machine Learning: **gradients**.

Topics covered:

* What gradients represent in a mathematical and intuitive sense
* How models learn through error minimization
* The role of gradient descent in training
* Why optimization is the core of learning algorithms

Understanding gradients is essential for going beyond “using models” and actually **understanding how they learn**.
